5.3 Ground Reaction Forces

When we exert a force onto the ground, the ground exerts an equal, opposing force called the ground reaction force (GRF). This force is thus a precise measurement of the amount of force applied by the athlete to the ground. In a laboratory setting GRF are measured using force plates, which is not feasible in a typical stadium. They can however be estimated from kinematics using the center of mass and Newtons second law (\(F=m*a\)).
